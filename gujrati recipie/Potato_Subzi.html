    Ingredients

  • 3-4 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnishing
  • Cooking oil

    Instruction

  1. Heat oil in a pan or kadai over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add the ginger paste and garlic paste. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes become soft and the oil starts to separate.
  5. Add the turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  6. Add the diced potatoes and mix them with the masala. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes start to get coated with the spices.
  7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan with a lid, and let the potatoes cook. You can sprinkle a little water if needed to prevent sticking. Cook until the potatoes are tender, stirring occasionally.
  8. Once the potatoes are cooked, add garam masala and mix well. Cook for another couple of minutes to let the flavors meld.
  9. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  10. Remove from heat and serve the Potato Subzi hot with roti, naan, or rice.
