    Ingredients

  • 6-8 ripe tomatoes
  • Salt to taste
  • Black pepper (optional)
  • Fresh herbs (such as basil or parsley) for added flavor (optional)

    Instruction

  1. Wash the Tomatoes: Rinse the tomatoes thoroughly under cold water to remove any dirt or debris.
  2. Blanch and Peel: Bring a pot of water to boil. Using a knife, make a small 'X' mark on the bottom of each tomato. Carefully drop the tomatoes into the boiling water for about 30-60 seconds or until you notice the skin starting to peel away from the 'X' mark.
  3. Ice Bath: Using a slotted spoon, transfer the blanched tomatoes immediately into a bowl of ice water. This helps stop the cooking process and makes it easier to peel the skin off. Once cooled, peel the skin off each tomato.
  4. Remove Seeds: Cut the tomatoes in half and gently squeeze out the seeds and excess juice. You can use a small spoon or your fingers for this step. Discard the seeds and juice.
  5. Blend: Place the peeled and deseeded tomatoes in a blender. Blend until smooth. If you prefer a chunkier texture, you can pulse the blender a few times.
  6. Strain (Optional): If you want a smoother consistency, strain the blended mixture through a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth to remove any remaining pulp or seeds.
  7. Season: Add salt to taste and a dash of black pepper if desired. You can also add fresh herbs like basil or parsley for extra flavor.
  8. Chill: Transfer the tomato juice to a pitcher and refrigerate for at least 1-2 hours before serving. Chilling allows the flavors to meld and the juice to become more refreshing.
  9. Serve: Once chilled, give the juice a good stir and serve in glasses. You can garnish with a sprig of fresh herbs if desired.
