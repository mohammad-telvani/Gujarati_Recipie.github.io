    Ingredients

  • 250 grams green chillies (choose a variety that suits your spice tolerance)
  • 2 tablespoons mustard seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1 teaspoon nigella seeds (kalonji)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chilli powder (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1/4 cup mustard oil (or any other oil of your choice)
  • 1/4 cup mustard oil (or any other oil of your choice)
  • Juice of 2-3 lemons

    Instruction

  1. Wash and dry the green chillies thoroughly. You can leave the stems intact or remove them, depending on your preference. Slit the chillies lengthwise, but be careful not to cut them into pieces.
  2. In a dry pan, lightly roast the mustard seeds, fennel seeds, fenugreek seeds, and nigella seeds. Roast them on low heat until they release a fragrant aroma. Be careful not to burn them.
  3. Allow the roasted seeds to cool down, and then grind them into a coarse powder using a mortar and pestle or a spice grinder.
  4. In a mixing bowl, combine the chopped green chillies, turmeric powder, red chilli powder, and the ground spice mix. Add salt as per your taste.
  5. Heat the mustard oil in a small pan until it reaches its smoking point. Allow the oil to cool down to room temperature.
  6. Pour the cooled oil over the chilli and spice mixture. Mix well to ensure all the chillies are coated with the oil and spices.
  7. Transfer the mixture to a clean, dry glass jar. Ensure that the jar is completely dry, as any moisture can lead to spoilage.
  8. Seal the jar tightly and store it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
