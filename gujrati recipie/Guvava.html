    Ingredients

  • Ripe guavas (3-4, depending on size)
  • Sugar (optional, to taste)
  • Water (1 cup)
  • Lemon juice (optional, for added flavor)

    Instruction

  1. Wash and peel the guavas: Rinse the guavas under cold water to remove any dirt or impurities. Cut off the ends of the guavas and then peel the skin using a knife or a peeler. You can choose to leave the skin on for added fiber and nutrients if you prefer.
  2. Remove the seeds: Cut the guavas into small pieces and scoop out the seeds with a spoon. The seeds can be quite hard and may affect the texture of the juice.
  3. Blend the guavas: Place the guava pieces in a blender. Add a little water (about 1/4 cup) to help with the blending process. Blend until you get a smooth puree.
  4. Strain the puree (optional): If you prefer a smoother juice without any pulp, strain the guava puree through a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth into a bowl. Use a spoon to press the puree and extract as much juice as possible.
  5. Prepare the guava juice: In a pitcher, combine the strained guava juice with the remaining water. You can adjust the water quantity according to your desired concentration of juice. If you prefer a sweeter juice, you can add sugar at this stage and mix until it's dissolved. You can also add a splash of lemon juice for a tangy kick.
  6. Chill and serve: Refrigerate the guava juice for a couple of hours to chill. When ready to serve, give the juice a good stir and pour it into glasses filled with ice. Garnish with a slice of guava or a twist of lemon, if desired.
  7. Enjoy: Your homemade guava juice is ready to be enjoyed! Sip and savor the refreshing and tropical flavor of guavas.
