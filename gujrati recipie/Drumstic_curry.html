    Ingredients

  • 4-5 drumsticks, washed and cut into 3-inch pieces
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

    Instruction

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. When they start to splutter, add asafoetida (hing) and curry leaves.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and the oil starts to separate.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  6. Add drumstick pieces to the spice mixture and mix well so that the drumsticks are coated with the spices.
  7. Add enough water to cover the drumsticks, bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it simmer until the drumsticks are cooked and tender. This may take around 15-20 minutes.
  8. Once the drumsticks are cooked, add garam masala and mix well.
  9. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. If the curry is too thick, you can add a little more water.
  10. Let the curry simmer for a few more minutes so that the flavors meld together
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  12. Serve the drumstick curry hot with steamed rice or Indian bread like roti or naan.
