    Ingredients

  • 2 cups gram flour (besan)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Water, as needed
  • Oil, for frying

    Instruction

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the gram flour, turmeric powder, red chili powder, carom seeds, asafoetida, salt, and 2 tablespoons of oil.
  2. Mix the dry ingredients well to incorporate the spices evenly into the flour.
  3. Gradually add water little by little and knead the mixture into a firm dough. The dough should not be too soft or too hard.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan on medium heat.
  5. While the oil is heating up, take a portion of the dough and place it in a sev press or a gathiya maker. Choose a plate with medium-sized holes for making the gathiya.
  6. Once the oil is hot, press the sev press directly over the hot oil in a circular motion, making sure the dough strands are evenly distributed. Be careful not to overcrowd the pan.
  7. Fry the gathiya in batches until they turn golden brown and crispy. Stir them occasionally to ensure even frying.
  8. Once the gathiya are cooked, remove them using a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to absorb any excess oil.
  9. Allow the fried gathiya to cool completely before storing them in an airtight container.
  10. Repeat the process with the remaining dough until all the gathiya are fried.
