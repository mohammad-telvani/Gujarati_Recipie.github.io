    Ingredients

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled, pitted, and chopped
  • 1 cup yogurt (preferably sour)
  • 2 tablespoons besan (gram flour)
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 4-5 curry leaves
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Sugar or jaggery (optional, to balance the flavors)

    Instruction

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk the yogurt until smooth. Add the besan (gram flour) and turmeric powder to the yogurt and whisk again until well combined. This mixture is known as "khatta" and forms the base for fajeto.
  2. Heat oil in a deep pan or pot over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them crackle.
  3. Add asafoetida, dried red chilies, and curry leaves to the pan. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the chopped mangoes to the pan and sauté for a couple of minutes, allowing them to soften slightly.
  5. Pour the yogurt-besan mixture (khatta) into the pan and stir well to combine with the mangoes.
  6. Allow the mixture to come to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. This helps cook the besan and blend the flavors.
  7. Check the consistency of the fajeto. If it's too thick, you can add a little water to achieve the desired consistency. Adjust the salt and sugar/jaggery if needed.
  8. Serve the fajeto hot with steamed rice or as a soup.
