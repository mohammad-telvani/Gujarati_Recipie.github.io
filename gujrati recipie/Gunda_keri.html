    Ingredients

  • 10-12 bird's eye chilies (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 2-3 raw mangoes, firm and unripe
  • 1/4 cup mustard oil (for pickle flavor, you can use any other oil if preferred)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons fenugreek (methi) seeds, roasted and ground
  • 1 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tablespoons salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon nigella seeds (kalonji)

    Instruction

  1. Wash and dry the bird's eye chilies and raw mangoes thoroughly. Cut the mangoes into small pieces, discarding the pits. You can keep the skin on or peel it, depending on your preference.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the mango pieces, bird's eye chilies, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and set aside for about 2-3 hours. This will help the mangoes release some moisture.
  3. After the resting time, heat mustard oil in a pan until it reaches its smoking point. Turn off the heat and let it cool down to room temperature.
  4. Once the oil has cooled, add roasted and ground fenugreek seeds and asafoetida. Mix well.
  5. Heat a small pan and dry roast mustard seeds, cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, and nigella seeds until aromatic. Grind them into a coarse powder.
  6. Add the ground spice powder to the mango and chili mixture. Mix in the prepared oil with fenugreek and asafoetida.
  7. Transfer the pickle mixture into clean, dry glass jars. Press it down firmly to remove any air pockets.
  8. Ensure that the pickle is submerged under the oil to prevent spoilage. You might need to top up with more oil if required.
  9. Seal the jars tightly and place them in a sunny spot for about 5-7 days. Shake the jars gently every day to ensure the spices are evenly distributed.
  10. After the pickling process, store the Gunda Keri Achar in a cool, dark place. It will develop better flavor over time and can be consumed after about 2-3 weeks.
