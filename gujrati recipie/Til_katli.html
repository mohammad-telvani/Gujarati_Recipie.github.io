    Ingredients

  • 1 cup white sesame seeds
  • 1 cup jaggery or sugar
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder (optional)
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, etc.) for garnishing (optional)

    Instruction

  1. Dry Roasting Sesame Seeds: Heat a pan over medium heat and add the sesame seeds. Dry roast the sesame seeds while continuously stirring. Roast until they turn golden brown and release a nutty aroma. Be careful not to burn them. This should take around 5-7 minutes. Once roasted, remove the sesame seeds from the pan and set them aside.
  2. Preparing Jaggery Syrup: In the same pan, add the jaggery (or sugar) along with a little water (about 1/4 cup) to dissolve it. Cook the jaggery (or sugar) over medium heat, stirring continuously until it melts and forms a syrup. You can check the syrup consistency by dropping a small amount into a bowl of water. It should form a firm ball when rolled between your fingers.
  3. Mixing Sesame Seeds: Once the jaggery syrup reaches the desired consistency, add the roasted sesame seeds to the pan. Mix the sesame seeds well with the jaggery syrup, ensuring they are coated evenly.
  4. Adding Ghee and Flavoring: Add the ghee (clarified butter) to the mixture and mix well. The ghee adds richness and helps bind the mixture together. If you're using cardamom powder for flavor, add it now and mix again.
  5. Shaping the Katli: Immediately transfer the mixture to a greased plate or a flat surface lined with parchment paper. Using a rolling pin or the back of a greased spoon, flatten the mixture to your desired thickness. You can also use a greased flat-bottomed bowl to achieve an even surface.
  6. Garnishing and Cutting: While the mixture is still warm, you can optionally press chopped nuts onto the surface for garnishing. Allow the mixture to cool slightly but not completely. While it's still warm, cut it into diamond or square shapes using a sharp knife.
  7. Cooling and Storing: Let the Til Katli cool completely and firm up before removing the pieces from the plate. Once fully cooled, carefully separate the individual pieces and store them in an airtight container.
