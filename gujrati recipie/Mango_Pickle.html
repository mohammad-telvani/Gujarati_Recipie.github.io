    Ingredients

  • 2-3 medium-sized ripe mangoes
  • 1/4 cup mustard oil (you can also use any other vegetable oil)
  • 2 tablespoons mustard seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Asafoetida (hing), a pinch
  • 1 teaspoon nigella seeds (kalonji)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • Jaggery or sugar (optional, for balancing flavors)
  • Clean, dry glass jars for storing the pickle

    Instruction

  1. Wash and thoroughly dry the mangoes. Peel them and cut into small bite-sized pieces. Discard the seeds.
  2. In a dry pan, lightly roast the fenugreek seeds, mustard seeds, and fennel seeds until they release their aroma. Grind these roasted seeds into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle
  3. Heat the mustard oil in a pan until it starts to smoke. Turn off the heat and allow it to cool slightly. This step is important to remove any strong raw oil smell.
  4. Once the oil has cooled, add the asafoetida, nigella seeds, chopped ginger, and chopped garlic. Saut√© them for a couple of minutes until fragrant.
  5. Add the roasted spice powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt to the oil mixture. Mix well to form a spice paste.
  6. Add the mango pieces to the spice paste and mix thoroughly, ensuring all the mango pieces are coated with the spice mixture.
  7. Let the mango mixture sit for a few hours, allowing the flavors to meld. You can cover it with a clean cloth during this time.
  8. Heat a little more oil in a pan until it smokes, then turn off the heat and let it cool slightly. This is known as "tempering."
  9. Seal the jars tightly and store them in a cool, dry place. The pickle will develop its flavors over time and can be consumed after a few days. Remember to use a dry spoon every time you take out the pickle to prevent spoilage.
