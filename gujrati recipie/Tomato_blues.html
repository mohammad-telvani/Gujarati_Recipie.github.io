    Ingredients

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as carrots, peas, potatoes, beans, cauliflower), chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste

    Instruction

  1. Heat oil in a pan or kadai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the spices are well incorporated into the tomato mixture.
  6. Add the chopped mixed vegetables to the pan. Mix everything together.
  7. Cover the pan with a lid and let the vegetables cook on low to medium heat. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  8. Once the vegetables are tender, sprinkle garam masala and mix well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  9. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed
  10. Garnish with chopped cilantro leaves.
  11. Serve the Tomato and Vegetable Subzi hot with roti, chapati, naan, or rice.
