    Ingredients

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1/4 cup urad dal flour (split black gram flour)
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil (for the dough)
  • Water (as needed to make the dough)

    Instruction

  1. In a mixing bowl, add the gram flour, turmeric powder, red chili powder, ajwain, sesame seeds, and salt. Mix well to combine all the dry ingredients.
  2. Gradually add water in small amounts and knead the mixture into a firm and smooth dough. The dough should not be too soft or too sticky. It should be similar to the consistency of dough used for making pooris.
  3. Take a small portion of the dough and roll it between your palms to make a thin cylindrical shape. Place this on a greased plate or surface.
  4. Repeat this process with the remaining dough, making several cylindrical shapes.
  5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. To test if the oil is hot enough, drop a small piece of dough into the oil â€“ if it sizzles and rises to the surface, the oil is ready.
  6. Carefully slide a few cylindrical dough pieces into the hot oil. Fry them on medium heat, turning occasionally, until they turn golden brown and crisp. Make sure not to overcrowd the pan, as it can affect the frying process.
  7. Once fried, use a slotted spoon to remove the chorafali from the oil and drain them on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  8. Allow the chorafali to cool completely before storing them in an airtight container. They will continue to crisp up as they cool.
  9. Repeat the frying process with the remaining dough pieces until all the chorafali are fried.
  10. Your homemade chorafali is ready to be enjoyed! Serve them as a snack on their own or with chutneys of your choice.
